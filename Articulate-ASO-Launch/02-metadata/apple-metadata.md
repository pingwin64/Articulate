## Description (~3,700/4,000 characters)
```
Most vocabulary apps hand you a flashcard and hope for the best.

Articulate takes a different approach: you learn new words by reading real text, one word at a time.

It's called RSVP reading. Words appear one at a time, at your own pace, in a focused, distraction-free flow. No scrolling. No wandering eyes. Just one word, then the next.

When you see a word you don't know, tap it. See the definition instantly. Save it to your word bank. Keep reading.

After you finish, take a comprehension quiz to prove you understood. Then review your saved words with smart flashcards — or record yourself pronouncing them and get AI-powered accuracy scoring.

That's the loop: Read, discover, save, review, prove.

Finally, a vocabulary app built around real reading.


WHAT'S INSIDE

- 30+ curated texts — stories, science, philosophy, speeches, poetry, and more
- Adjustable reading speed — go at your own pace
- Instant definitions — tap any word while reading
- AI pronunciation drills — record yourself, get scored
- Spaced repetition flashcards that prioritize what you need most
- Comprehension quizzes after every text
- Upload your own text — paste or scan with camera
- Daily streaks, word goals, and weekly challenges
- Multiple fonts including OpenDyslexic for dyslexia
- 5-level progression — unlock new themes as you read


WHO READS WITH ARTICULATE

- People who want to build focus and replace doom-scrolling with something meaningful
- Readers with ADHD or dyslexia who need a calm, one-word-at-a-time display
- English learners building vocabulary and pronunciation through real reading
- Students preparing for SAT, GRE, TOEFL, or IELTS
- Anyone looking for a mindful daily reading ritual — not another noisy app
- Self-improvement readers who want a small habit that compounds


WHY IT WORKS

Traditional vocabulary apps teach words in isolation — a definition here, a quiz there. But learning words in context leads to deeper, longer-lasting retention.

Articulate puts context first. You encounter words inside real stories, articles, and speeches. You see how they're used. You save the ones that matter. Then you review and practice pronunciation — building skills that carry into real conversations and real reading.


FREE TO START

The free tier includes core reading features, 3 text categories, daily streaks, and limited lookups. Premium unlocks unlimited definitions, pronunciation drills, all 30+ texts, every font and theme, custom uploads, and streak freezes.

Start reading. Start learning. One word at a time.

Questions or feedback? admin@ordco.net
```

## Keyword Strategy & Rationale

### Keywords Included (100/100 characters used)
```
speed,reading,vocabulary,fluency,dyslexia,esl,focus,comprehension,study,learn,words,rsvp,education
```

### Why These Keywords:

**High-Value Search Terms:**
- `speed` + `reading` = "speed reading" (high volume, core feature)
- `vocabulary` (broad, high-intent)
- `fluency` (ESL/language learning audience)
- `rsvp` (niche but zero competition, describes unique mechanic)

**Audience-Specific:**
- `dyslexia` (underserved market, OpenDyslexic font is differentiator)
- `esl` (English as Second Language - major market)
- `comprehension` (students/test prep)
- `study` + `learn` (education intent)

**Feature Keywords:**
- `focus` (distraction-free reading angle)
- `words` (vocabulary context)
- `education` (category reinforcement)

### Keywords Intentionally EXCLUDED:
- Brand names (against Apple guidelines)
- Plurals (Apple auto-includes: "word" also matches "words")
- Duplicates from title/subtitle ("read", "vocab", "builder" already in subtitle)
- Low-value generic terms ("app", "books", "text")

### Keyword Optimization Notes:
- **No spaces after commas** (verified: "speed,reading" not "speed, reading")
- Exactly 100 characters (maximum allowed)
- 13 discrete keywords
- Balanced between broad appeal (vocabulary) and niche positioning (rsvp, dyslexia)
